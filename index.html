Weekend update - Squashing bugs and Fixing Terrain

Here is the weekend update for The Holler(Still Working on the Title), Lots of bugs got squashed this week so it is hard to keep up. The main ones wer fixes to the Terrain, as it was getting clamped at the height limit and thus was a plateau on some of the map, which is not ideal. Also I looked into an automated A* Pathing algo for finding paths for the player. This had some limited success but is useful. I have been trying to avoid creating the paths by hand but I fear it is unavoidable at this point.

One of the biggest issues with the algorithm is finding the balance between shortest distance and least intense hills. The wighting of both factors is difficult to nail down, but I am getting better at doing the scuplting on my own. Pinch has been particularly useful to widen paths slightly. There are definitly going to be some sketch paths to drive down in the game, not least of which is the one that gets you from the plateau to the main base.

No new features have really been added since the UTV, but I am making progress. Getting the paths made is going to be important for nailing down the gameplay loop. I hope to have a solid loop by the end of this week, however that may be difficult. Shooting to have a playable product by Late July to Mid August. It may not be pretty but it will work. On another note, it is quite odd to be driving the UTV around without any sound effects, thats definiely somethign that can be amended soon. I may just go record my own car to get a baseline loop.

There is a severe lack of sounds everywhere, I think that could be a big lever to pull and just get some simple sounds like walking around, picking up items, or interacting with the terminals. That and actually being able to drop you backpack from your inventory. Or being able to place items and hold them in your hands, which will be very important for the core loop of the game.

UTV and SSAO

The UTV got some more work done on it. The model that I will use for the forseeable near future was made and while it is crude, its not a box. In doing this I found a serious issue with the lighting that was pissing me off. SSAO or Screen Space Ambient Occulsion was cranked way too high by defaut, and it took me an hour to find the fix. Now it is much better. But it was driving me insane. Also the UTV has lights. Theyre not the brightest but they work.

The UTV has been tuned. It will use AWD for now. I was going to do RWD with an AWD toggle, but for building the map I need AWD standard. The hill climbing has been kept semi reasonable. It doenst start slipping until 30degrees which is fine, and the Torgue per wheel is 500. There is a toggle to take it from AWD to RWD, and this is easy enough to wire in later, but for now its just there, dormant.

FreeCAD has been working pretty well for making these models. Despite them being crude, that is ok. Later I will use blender, especially for any more complex Geometries, but this has been phenomenal. Tommorow I tackle paths. Currently debating how to do that but I do like just going through and making them with the color coded grades.

2026 · 06 · 23 LOG 005

Inventory System Established

I spent this evening building out the inventory system. It is a derivation of the Escape from Tarkov inventory system with a grid system where you can rotate items and you can swap out packs on your back for different ones. This is not an uncommon system, and there is not much new or really any that has been introduced but it should be a nice addition to the game.

Each item that you can put in your inventory and every backpack is a physics item, it can be placed in the world, moved around with G, picked up with R, or opened with E if it is a container. The container view opens your invenotry on the left and the container on the right, which is all well and good but I am debating where to put the players inventory screen when not looking at an external inventory. Its simple enough to keep it like it is, but its odd looking.

Most of the bugs encountered when building extended from locking the cursor during inventory managment and unlocking it after the window closes. That and there was one amusing bug where if you dropped an item from your inventory the scale was dropped to .1 of the original, so the placeholder box became a small cube, then if you picked it up and dropped it again, it became a grain of sand. All sorted though. Next is making the inventory UI look a bit better because the current look of it is a bit archaic and while the visuals are placeholders, it could look better for verification.

2026 · 06 · 21 LOG 004

Made the sun obey the laws of physics

Before now, the sun was just following a straight line from 90 degrees to 270 degrees, peaking with an altitude at zenith. Because I chose West Virginia as the basis for where the game takes place I decided to to make the sun pass with a realistic path through the sky for that lat depending on the day selected. So on the summer solstice, the sun passes as high as it could and the day is as long as it would ever be. For the winter solstice, its low and the day is short.

This has all been verified with a city near 38.2 degrees Lat which is where the orignal landscape is based. The sun sets and rises from the correct az, and rises to the correct elevation. This was not a necessary addition but it does add to the beauty of the world, and it makes winters much more dangerous as most of the day is spent in darkness. The sun also casts a realistic sunset. We can adjust this later, especially when weather systems are introduced. Currently it is jsut red and orange, but it can very quickly be adjusted to include purples and pinks.

One fun bug that eventually became frustrating was the sky darkening way too much before sunset. Tuned and fixed, but it was perplexing.

2026 · 06 · 19 LOG 002

A real valley, walked at one-to-one

Pulled real USGS elevation data of the Greenbrier valley near Kennison, West Virginia. Pushed it into QGIS to resample it with cubic spline, exported it then applied it to the terrain in the Unity Engine. it has a 1000ft elevation change from the valley to the peaks with a dynamic lanscape.

The landscape is a mix between folded mountains and a plateau. This can introduce some interesting mechanics later and rewards careful planning and understanding of the map. THe base is currently just on the spur in the middle of the map.
