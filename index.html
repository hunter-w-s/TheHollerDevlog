Main Menu and Save System

Today was almost entirely based around the most difficult mechanic yet, that being the save system. To properly build it I had to make it so every not static or non configurable object had a saveID that could be saved. Then built a main menu. Then build out the load save system. This took the better part of the evening, but that is mostly my fault. This should have been done weeks ago but it kept slipping my mind.

As much as I would love to dig into th emany bugs and many hitches I ran into in this process, I am out of gas. The main points here are that this is a huge system and for obvious reasons I feel was worth doing correctly the first time, rather than having to rebuild it later to account for changing systems. Every part of it is modular. This will be tough to fully test out and verify. But every bug is fixable. People do it every time they make a savable game and there is no reason I can't now.

I don't know how else to express the difficulty of this implmentation. Props to every developer that came before me that had put all their responses in Stack Overlow, Reddit, and online lit. They really had to do some heavy lifting, I just piggybacked by reading that.

2026 · 07 · 12 LOG 016

Much needed break and incremental progress

I took a much needed break from this project this week. It wasn't a super long break but it allowed me to think about what I wanted to do next. Once I was back I made a few small additions. They break down into 3 main features. A zoom for the camera, a slot for the backpack, glass material and a door.

A few of these are self explantatory. The zoom uses the X key by default and scales down the sensitivity when you use it. The glass material for the windows and UTV windscreen. Eventually it will have baked reflections on static windows, but I am not sure about that one yet. The more interesting additions are the slot for the back pack and the door. The backpack was still relatively simple. In the same way that we have a slot for hands and belt, we have a slot for the backpack so that you can move it from there to exterior containers and we have disallowed recursive backpacks. No stacking backpacks together. And maybe its not realistic that the backpack can store a bunch of stuff and take up less space in the exterior container than the total volume, but that's ok.

Lastly the door. I initially went with a fully physics interactable and draggable door. But quickly realized that a lot of people would probably dislike how clunky it was. So we went with scripted opening using the hinge join component, scripted to open to 90 degrees. What's nice is this has a spring component so it opens kind of realistically. It also has lock, which for horror elements later will be pretty interesting. It all runs through the interactable script that we've used on so much else which is great for seamless usage. Eventually leaving the door unlocked will have consequences. Creatures coming in through an unlocked door. Also I had some more ideas for creatures and interactions. For one. If you take a picture of a skinwalker in the field with the flash on, it will trigger it to transform and chase you down. Of course this will be something you can recognize before hand, but I can already imagine the horror as a wolf twists itself into a semi humanoid and chases the players.

Inventory Flesh Out and A Usable UTV Bed

I did some much needed work on the Inventory system. Now you have a drop key so you can hover over items and hit R to drop them. Or you can drag them to a drop zone and they will be dropped. We also added the belt slots for small items such as flashlights, or bear spray or whatever else we add later. There is now also a hands slot so you can quickly put stuff into your hands with F. This will then allow you to place things or just carry bulk items that wont fit in your backpack. This system still needs a fair amount of work but the bones are there and the next things are a hot bar and rendering the thing the player is carrying. Right now its invisible, which for obvious reasons isnt exactly what we want.

On the topic of the UTV, there is now both a brake light, so the player can see on the dash exactly when the handbrake is applied. Great since theres not an audio queue yet. Additionally you can now lock things into the bed of the UTV. In the future this will be rendered with rachet straps going over the item in the bed, but for now. It just disables the rigid body and locks it to the UTV's location and rotation. Its very nice to be able to say load up a storage container or a chainsaw and be able to take it with you across the map.

Placing cameras is next, or rather just placing anything, because right now you have to go into your inventory to drop items. But the player should have as little friction as we can get while still keeping the feel of the game.

2026 · 07 · 07 LOG 013

Rotating, Pause Menu, UTV and More

Here is a summation of all that has been changed since the last log. Firstly now the player can rotate objects with physics by holding right click and moving their mouse. Kind of hard to fix your UTV if it flips and you can rotate it. In the same vein of the UTV, now the player one exit clipped into the ground. If the UTV had flipped on its left side, the exit point would be under the terrain and thus they would fall through the world, which isnt exactly intended. There is a known bug right now where the F key controls the headlights on the UTV, regardless of whether or not you actually are seated, also not intended. There is now another smoothing layer added onto the camera in the UTV so that the stutter that had presisted through interpolating the rigidbody is muted.

There is now also a pause menu the player can access with escape. Right now it has 2 buttons, resume and exit, but there will be plenty more added later. BEcause we introduced a pause menu, it was a good time to consolidate the controllers so that we arent fighting race conditions on which script controls the cursor. I have also fixed the scaling on the inventory screen, it was a simple matter of scaling to the screen rather than pixel and adjusting some rect transforms.

We are making some very good progress. Playtesters have been giving good feedback and expressed displeasure with how weak the UTV feels. While I originally designed it to be intended for specific grade limits, I am not so certain now. If it is actually unpleasant to drive because its weak, it becomes a friction point, even if it is accurate to real life. We are getting closer and closer to a releaseable build.

2026 · 07 · 05 LOG 012

More Trees and Optimizing the Trees

A tough decision was made today to go with a simplifed cone tree for our spruce tree, you can see this in the plate section. I really did like the old tree with all of its detail, but it was dragging performance down way too much to be worth it. With 171k trees on the map, we cannot afford to have complex shadows and massive amounts of objects draw in. Right now we are sitting at an avg of 2% frames over budget. which i would say is workable. and even when they are over budget, it isnt spiking down too much. And this is on a lax GPU. Most of the spikes are on the CPU but it only clocks in at 3% of frames. Still shippable.

Now that that is actually solved and not just somewhat solved, we can move onto the gameplay loop. We have everything we need to get that nailed down and I legitimatly think we will have a playable game by next week.

2026 · 07 · 02 LOG 010

Tenth LOG. Paths Nearing Completion. And More

This marks 10 logs. This is by far the furthest I have gotten on a game and I'm going to keep going. It has been a succesful 3 days. The paths are nearing completion and the only things really left to do are thicken them up so they look realistic, but for now the main paths are done. Extras may be added later, but not too many as that plays into the next point. Data Scoring.

The Player is tasked with collecting data. Naturally if data was valued the same whether it was collected near base or far away, nobody would ever explore. Data collected far from roads and far from base will get a muliplier applied to this. This introduces a inherent reward system to traveling and reaching out further. The values I settled on wher a road weight of 1.167 and a base weight of .887. This works great as the player is more rewarded for going into the woods rather than just sprinting to the edge of the map. We will also be introducing a heat effect to the game, meaning if you visit an area too much the value of those photos will be lower.

Back on the UTV, there is now a camera smoothing script applied because as it stood, even I was getting motion sick looking at the screen, and I know someone who would have vomited. Looking at you R. While I think it looks good now, R is going to be the first play tester so that the motion can be tuned correctly. Because I've been staring at this scene so much I can not be trusted to actually make that judgement. There is also now a barrier. This will be diegetic later, but for now it is an editor only view and the player will be stopped. Later they will be picked up by an entity and teleported back to base. Punishing, yes, but make sure you look at the warning signs. Most likely it will be flagged trees and the ambient sounds going quiet to warn you from going further, before you get snached.

Inventory System Established

I spent this evening building out the inventory system. It is a derivation of the Escape from Tarkov inventory system with a grid system where you can rotate items and you can swap out packs on your back for different ones. This is not an uncommon system, and there is not much new or really any that has been introduced but it should be a nice addition to the game.

Each item that you can put in your inventory and every backpack is a physics item, it can be placed in the world, moved around with G, picked up with R, or opened with E if it is a container. The container view opens your invenotry on the left and the container on the right, which is all well and good but I am debating where to put the players inventory screen when not looking at an external inventory. Its simple enough to keep it like it is, but its odd looking.

Most of the bugs encountered when building extended from locking the cursor during inventory managment and unlocking it after the window closes. That and there was one amusing bug where if you dropped an item from your inventory the scale was dropped to .1 of the original, so the placeholder box became a small cube, then if you picked it up and dropped it again, it became a grain of sand. All sorted though. Next is making the inventory UI look a bit better because the current look of it is a bit archaic and while the visuals are placeholders, it could look better for verification.

2026 · 06 · 21 LOG 004

Made the sun obey the laws of physics

Before now, the sun was just following a straight line from 90 degrees to 270 degrees, peaking with an altitude at zenith. Because I chose West Virginia as the basis for where the game takes place I decided to to make the sun pass with a realistic path through the sky for that lat depending on the day selected. So on the summer solstice, the sun passes as high as it could and the day is as long as it would ever be. For the winter solstice, its low and the day is short.

This has all been verified with a city near 38.2 degrees Lat which is where the orignal landscape is based. The sun sets and rises from the correct az, and rises to the correct elevation. This was not a necessary addition but it does add to the beauty of the world, and it makes winters much more dangerous as most of the day is spent in darkness. The sun also casts a realistic sunset. We can adjust this later, especially when weather systems are introduced. Currently it is jsut red and orange, but it can very quickly be adjusted to include purples and pinks.

One fun bug that eventually became frustrating was the sky darkening way too much before sunset. Tuned and fixed, but it was perplexing.

2026 · 06 · 19 LOG 002

A real valley, walked at one-to-one

Pulled real USGS elevation data of the Greenbrier valley near Kennison, West Virginia. Pushed it into QGIS to resample it with cubic spline, exported it then applied it to the terrain in the Unity Engine. it has a 1000ft elevation change from the valley to the peaks with a dynamic lanscape.

The landscape is a mix between folded mountains and a plateau. This can introduce some interesting mechanics later and rewards careful planning and understanding of the map. THe base is currently just on the spur in the middle of the map.
